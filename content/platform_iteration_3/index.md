# Platform
Whereas a mockup prototype of our platform was created in AdobeXD for the midterm demoday, for communicational purposes. It was chosen to make a more functional interactive app with the help of Unity. The SDK of Mapbox was implemented in Unity and a first version of our prototype worked. You could actually see yourself walking around in your neighbourhood. A first implementation of buttons (build button and profile page) were added but not further developed after our coach did recommend to focus on evaluation which could better be done via the application of a more lo-fi and easy to make prototype.

A meeting with Regina steered us into a different direction. Our focus shifted from realizing to actually validating our system. We discussed the main points we wanted to communicate about our project for both the platform and the building system.
For the platform this was mainly to show its core functionality and aesthetics. Therefore focusing on a more simple realization method in AdobeXD would be more appropriate choice to realize these goals.

The first version focused on visualizing the main functionality of the app. The map itself, what and where the most important buttons were and how we could best visualize ideas built by users of the app. This version however lacked a lot of hierarchy, contrast and overall aesthetics. This means respectively that many of the buttons and images feel like they are simply put underneath each other and don't belong together, that the colors are too similar and it does not look professional at all.

In a later version, the contrast of colors was increased, the hierarchy among visuals was improved and the total appearance was more professional. Still, the fully white screen with colored buttons feels incoherent. Furthermore, the pointer for indicating clustered ideas has been updated.

The latest version has way more contrast between colors which are moreover more saturated, hierarchy is improved which makes the app feel more like a whole. This is mainly visible at the pages of individual ideas and the achievement page for example. The app looks professional and feels like a app that could be real.
