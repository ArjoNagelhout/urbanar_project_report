The process started off really well. Meetings were clearly organized, structured and conducted. Minutes were kept in order to keep track of all tasks, as well as a GitHub repository was added.

However, the collaboration went downhill after a couple of weeks. Because of the online communication, it became harder to keep track of the work and progress of other group members. Many parts of ideation where therefore conducted behind closes doors, which left other group members feeling overruled. 

In the a more structured way of working was found. The core parts of the project, such as the development of the platform and the app were clearly seperated and allowed parts of the team to split tasks. Updates about the progress was constantly shared and help could be provided if desired. This way, one person could completely focus on realizing the build system an the other could fully focus on the platform. The results turned out great and both prototypes work as they should.
